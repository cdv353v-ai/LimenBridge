The gap between sessions
is where clients lose ground.

Every therapist knows this pattern: a client does solid work in session — and comes back three days later having lost most of it. Not because the therapy isn't working. Because nothing holds the ground between sessions. LimenBridge is built for that gap.

The problem it solves

168 hours in a week. One hour in the therapy room. That leaves 167 hours during which the nervous system defaults to its habitual baseline — often a baseline of chronic low-level alert.

"Between sessions, there is no structure. No predictable cue that tells the nervous system: you are safe now, the work continues. The body, left without that signal, reverts."

This between-session deterioration is well-documented in clinical literature. Clients return having partially or fully reset. Sessions begin with rebuilding rather than advancing. Progress slows.

LimenBridge addresses this directly — not by replacing therapy, but by providing daily nervous system support on the days you are not there.

What LimenBridge is

A short daily audio program — morning and evening — built around the chronobiology of the autonomic nervous system.

Morning track

Works with the Cortisol Awakening Response — the natural hormonal shift 30–45 minutes after waking. Gentle, grounding, designed to give the activation a safe shape rather than suppress it.

Evening track

Supports the shift toward parasympathetic dominance. Slower, deeper. Helps the body complete the transition that accumulated tension and screen exposure tend to interrupt.

28-day structure

Grounded in UCL habit formation research (Lally et al.). The same 14 tracks repeated intentionally across 28 days — not for lack of material, but because the second listen happens with a nervous system that has already begun to change. Days 29–30 are a structured reflection pause, not a gap.

Mood tracking

A brief morning and evening check-in — a single score, an optional note. After day 4, a 28-day graph becomes visible. Clients can bring this to sessions as a simple record of their week.

Scientific basis

Habit formation — Lally et al., UCL (2010) Automaticity in health behaviors develops over 18–254 days, with the steepest gains in the first weeks of consistent practice. The 28-day structure is built around this curve.
HRV and audio intervention Research supports the use of structured auditory stimuli in modulating autonomic tone and improving heart rate variability — a key marker of nervous system regulation.
Cortisol Awakening Response The CAR is a reproducible biological phenomenon. Morning interventions timed to this window have documented effects on perceived stress and daytime cortisol levels.
Between-session deterioration Clinically observed and documented across multiple modalities of psychotherapy. Structured between-session support has been shown to improve treatment outcomes and reduce regression.

Safety and scope

LimenBridge is not a clinical tool. It does not diagnose, treat, or substitute for professional care.

It is a wellness audio program designed as structural support between sessions — analogous to a sleep hygiene practice or a breathing exercise, not to a therapeutic intervention.

It is not appropriate for clients in acute crisis, active suicidal ideation, or those requiring clinical stabilisation. For all other clients in ongoing therapy, it presents no contraindications we are aware of — but we encourage therapists to make that assessment themselves, which is precisely why we offer free access.
