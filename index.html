How it works

Two states.
Two tracks.

The way you feel throughout the day isn't random. It follows a biological logic. LimenBridge is built around it: the morning track and the evening track are different, because morning and evening are different states of your body.

☼

Morning

So the day doesn't start with anxiety already waiting

About thirty minutes after waking, the body shifts out of sleep and into the day. This is a natural rise — not stress. The morning track is gentle, no sharp sounds. It works with that moment.

☾

Evening

So you fall asleep lighter

By evening the body is ready to shift into recovery. But accumulated tension, screens, and news disrupt that. The evening track is slower, deeper. It helps the shift happen.

From Mark

Why this exists

A few years ago, I couldn't sleep properly. I'd lie there staring at the ceiling, replaying the day over and over. Strange, considering I help people with anxiety every day. But I couldn't fix my own.

One evening my wife said, "Listen, I found something — breathing exercises with specific music, guided by a voice." I was skeptical. But within a week, sleep started coming back.

And here's what really struck me. I work with people who are in therapy. An hour a week — real work, real breakthroughs. Then the session ends, and they're on their own. We did the math — that's roughly 167 hours until the next one.

And in those hours, the same thing happens that happened to me. Anxiety creeps back in the evening. Mornings feel heavy. Thoughts won't let go.

Therapy isn't meant to fill all that time — that's not its job. But between sessions, people need something to hold onto. The thing that helped me.

That's how LimenBridge came to be.

Mark Vdovskikh

Before you start

LimenBridge is built for a specific moment. It works best when you know who it's for — and who it isn't.

For

People in psychotherapy who want gentle structure between sessions — a way to hold the work in the body when the room is no longer there.

Not for

Acute crisis or active suicidal ideation. If you are in distress, please reach out to your therapist or your local emergency services.

Questions before you decide

Should I tell my therapist I'm using this?

Yes — and we'd encourage it. Your therapist knows your rhythm and can give you the most useful feedback on whether LimenBridge is helping, or whether your time between sessions might be better spent another way.

How is this different from Calm or Headspace?

Calm and Headspace are general meditation apps — meditation, sleep stories, gratitude practices, breath work, focus music. They're built for anyone, for any moment.

LimenBridge does one thing: it supports your nervous system on the days between psychotherapy sessions, with a structured morning and evening track that follows the body's biological rhythm. No menus, no choices to make — the right track arrives at the right time.

What's actually inside the program?

A 14-day cycle of morning and evening audio sessions, each built on guided breathing and specific musical structures. You also get a simple progress graph that begins on day four — so the first three days have no measurement, only listening.

Where is my data stored?

Your listening history and mood check-ins are stored locally, on your device. We don't keep a database of them, sell data, or run ads. If you switch devices or clear your browser, that history won't carry over — a synced version is on our roadmap.

What if it doesn't help me?

The first three days are free, with no card required — that's specifically so you can find out without committing. After that, you can cancel at any time directly from your account.
